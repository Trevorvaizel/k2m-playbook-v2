# Adversarial Review: Copies vs. Attention Design Gears

**Brutal honesty about conversion optimization**

---

## The 5 Gears (Recap)

From our attention framework:

1. **Friction Analysis** – Where will they bounce? Remove it.
2. **Entry Design** – Tiny door, not castle gates
3. **Cognitive Load** – Can they grasp it in 3 seconds?
4. **Visual Hierarchy** – Eye journey designed deliberately
5. **Mental Timeline** – Urgency without panic (inevitability, not threat)

---

## Adversarial Analysis

### Gear 1: Friction Analysis

**What works:**
✅ Opening line creates curiosity gap – no immediate resistance
✅ Story flows naturally – no jarring transitions
✅ No prerequisites or requirements mentioned upfront

**What creates friction:**
❌ **Word count on LinkedIn** – 300+ words before CTA = heavy commitment
❌ **No micro-commitments** – Jump straight from "here's the problem" to "join cohort"
❌ **Missing "try before you buy"** – No sample, no taste, no low-stakes entry

**The friction problem:**

> "Everyone uses ChatGPT. But not everyone knows how to use it. Here's the difference: Some students copy-paste answers..."

This is interesting. But then 200 words of explanation before any action.

**Alternative:** Reduce friction with micro-commitment

> "Everyone uses ChatGPT. But not everyone knows how to use it.
>
> Want to see which one you are? Take this 2-minute quiz: [link]
>
> Or jump straight to the cohort: [link]"

❌ **Current copy fails Gear 1** – Too much reading before choice point

---

### Gear 2: Entry Design

**What works:**
✅ Opening is relatable – "everyone uses ChatGPT" = familiar
✅ No technical jargon or prerequisites
✅ Curiosity gap creates desire to enter

**What creates friction:**
❌ **No "tiny door" option** – It's all or nothing (read everything → join cohort)
❌ **No sampling** – Can't taste the thinking before committing
❌ **No "try this first"** – Missing A.R.C. framework's "Reduce friction" step

**The entry design problem:**

The A.R.C. framework says:
- **A**ctivate tension ✓
- **R**educe friction ❌ (missing)
- **C**onvert ❌ (too much before conversion)

**Alternative:** Apply A.R.C. properly

> **ACTIVATE:**
> Everyone uses ChatGPT. But not everyone knows how to use it.
>
> **REDUCE:**
> Want to see the difference in 30 seconds?
> Try this: [quick exercise/challenge]
>
> **CONVERT:**
> That taste of thinking vs. copying? That's what the 6-week cohort teaches.
> Starts February 14. [CTA]

❌ **Current copy fails Gear 2** – Castle gate, not tiny door

---

### Gear 3: Cognitive Load (3-Second Rule)

**What works:**
✅ Opening line: "Everyone uses ChatGPT. But not everyone knows how" – graspable
✅ Headlines and subheads guide attention
✅ Short paragraphs create white space

**What creates overload:**
❌ **LinkedIn posts 250-300 words** – Too much for 3-second rule
❌ **Abstract concepts need unpacking** – "Understand what you're asking for" takes processing
❌ **No immediate "I get this"** – Requires reading to understand the value

**The cognitive load problem:**

3-second rule: Can they grasp the core value in 3 seconds?

**LinkedIn Post 1 opening:**
> "Everyone uses ChatGPT. But not everyone knows how to use it. Here's the difference: Some students copy-paste answers. They find a prompt that works. They use it for everything. Then they face a real problem..."

**Time to process:** ~8-10 seconds of reading

❌ **Current copy fails Gear 3** – Takes 8-10 seconds to grasp value

**Alternative:** Lead with the shift

> **HEADLINE:** Everyone uses ChatGPT. But not everyone knows how to use it.
>
> **The difference in one sentence:**
>
> Copiers look for prompts. Thinkers understand what they're asking for.
>
> [Then explain]

**New time:** 3-4 seconds to grasp the shift ✅

---

### Gear 4: Visual Hierarchy

**What works:**
✅ Paragraph breaks create rhythm
✅ Some posts use bullet points for scannability
✅ Bold text creates emphasis (in some versions)

**What creates problems:**
❌ **No clear visual hierarchy** – Wall of text on LinkedIn
❌ **No "eye journey" design** – Where should I look first?
❌ **Missing subheads in long-form** – No way to scan quickly

**The visual hierarchy problem:**

LinkedIn post structure:
```
[Hook paragraph]
[Story paragraph]
[Distinction paragraph]
[Cost paragraph]
[CTA paragraph]
```

**No visual markers** to guide the eye or allow scanning.

**Alternative:** Design the eye journey

> **HOOK:** Everyone uses ChatGPT. But not everyone knows how to use it.
>
> **THE PROBLEM:** [label]
> You copy-paste answers. Then university hits. You're stuck.
>
> **THE SHIFT:** [label]
> The question isn't "which prompts?" It's "do you understand what you're asking for?"
>
> **THE COST:** [label]
> The habits you form now will follow you into university.
>
> **THE CHOICE:** [label]
> Some arrive ready. Others figure it out when it matters.
>
> [CTA]

❌ **Current copy fails Gear 4** – No deliberate eye journey

---

### Gear 5: Mental Timeline

**What works:**
✅ "The habits you form now will follow you into university" – good inevitability
✅ "Some arrive ready. Others figure it out when matters" – good differentiation
✅ "Starts February 14" – concrete deadline

**What could be stronger:**
⚠️ **"6 weeks" is vague** – Doesn't create felt urgency
⚠️ **"Space for 200 students" is weak scarcity** – Not believable
⚠️ **Missing "act now because"** – No immediate trigger

**The mental timeline problem:**

> "The habits you form now will follow you into university."

This is good (inevitability). But it's distant. University is "someday."

**Alternative:** Add proximate timeline

> "The habits you form in the next 6 weeks will follow you into university in [number] months."
>
> "Some students are already signing up. 200 spots. When they're gone, they're gone."
>
> "Every week you wait, you're another week closer to university without having practiced thinking with AI."

✅ **Current copy partially passes Gear 5** – Good inevitability, weak immediacy

## The Honest Answer

**Do I think these copies are great to convert?**

**No.**

They're **great philosophy**. They're **great awareness-building**. They might even be **great brand-building**.

But they're **not conversion-optimized**.

**Why?**

1. **Too much friction** – No sampling, no micro-commitments
2. **Too much cognitive load** – Takes 8-10 seconds to grasp value
3. **No visual hierarchy** – Wall of text, no scannable structure
4. **No tiny entry door** – All-or-nothing commitment
5. **Weak immediate urgency** – Good future inevitability, no "act now because"

**What they ARE great for:**
- Top-of-funnel: "I never thought about it that way"
- Newsletter subscribers who already trust you
- People who read long-form (not scrollers)
- Building brand as "thoughtful AI education"

**What they NEED for conversion:**
- Landing page with visual hierarchy
- Video thumbnail (3-second hook)
- Micro-commitment quiz/assessment
- Sample lesson or exercise
- Tiered entry (free → paid → cohort)

## The Fix: Apply All 5 Gears

**If you want these to CONVERT, not just inspire:**

**Gear 1 (Friction):** Add "Are you a copier or thinker?" quiz
**Gear 2 (Entry):** Offer 2-minute read before cohort pitch
**Gear 3 (Load):** Lead with "Copy-pasters get stuck" headline
**Gear 4 (Hierarchy):** Use THE HOOK / THE TRAP / THE SHIFT structure
**Gear 5 (Timeline):** "47 spots claimed. February 14. Every week you wait..."
