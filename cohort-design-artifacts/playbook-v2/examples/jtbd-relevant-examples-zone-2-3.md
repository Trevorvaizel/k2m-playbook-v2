## University Decisions

### University Choice Examples
- UoN vs Strathmore (CS programs): "I'm interested in CS but worried I'm not good enough at math. UoN is larger and more competitive, Strathmore is smaller and more expensive but has good placement."
- Public vs Private: "Choosing between public university (affordable, large classes) and private university (expensive, smaller community, more personal attention)"
- Competitive vs Safe: "Should I apply to competitive 'reach' universities or play it safe with 'match' schools where I'm confident I'll get in?"
- Campus Culture: "I want a university with strong campus community. I don't want to be just a number at a huge school."
- Program Focus: "I'm interested in CS generally, but I want to focus on AI and machine learning specifically. How do different programs compare for that specialization?"

### Application Examples
- Essay Anxiety: "I'm stressed about writing my university application essays. I don't know how to stand out without sounding fake."
- Recommendation Letters: "I need to ask teachers for recommendation letters but I'm shy and haven't been very active in class."
- Multiple Acceptances: "I got accepted to three universities. I'm having trouble deciding between them because each has different strengths."

## Career Exploration

### Field Comparison Examples
- Software Engineering vs Data Science: "I love both coding and statistics. SE is more about building systems, DS is more about extracting insights. Which fits my personality better?"
- Passion vs Practicality: "My parents want me to pursue nursing (stable, respected) but I'm excited about graphic design (creative, less stable). How do I decide?"
- Healthcare Careers: "I think I want to work in healthcare but I'm not sure if I should be a doctor, nurse, or public health researcher."

### Career Discovery Examples
- /diverge for Exploration: "/diverge I think I want to work in healthcare but I'm not sure if I should be a doctor, nurse, or public health researcher. Help me see different angles."
- Strengths Assessment: "I'm good at math and I like helping people, but I don't want to work directly with patients. What healthcare careers fit that?"
- Industry Research: "/diverge I'm considering a career in tech but I don't know if I should be a developer, product manager, or data analyst. Help me explore."

### Decision-Making Examples
- /challenge for Testing: "/challenge I've decided to pursue computer science. Here's my reasoning: I love coding and it pays well. Help me see what I'm missing."
- Values Clarification: "I'm choosing between a high-paying job I'm less interested in vs. a lower-paying job I'm passionate about. How do I weigh this?"
- Long-Term Thinking: "I want a career that will still be relevant in 10 years. How do I future-proof my career choice?"

## Study Decisions

### Exam Preparation Examples
- Overwhelmed: "I have exams in 3 subjects at once (physics, math, chemistry). I'm most worried about physics but I feel overwhelmed by all of it."
- Learning Style: "Help me study for exams. I'm a visual learner but all my notes are text-heavy. I need diagrams, mind maps, visual techniques."
- Subject-Specific: "I'm studying for my physics exam and I keep confusing the concepts of voltage and current. Can you help me understand the difference?"
- Time Management: "I have 2 weeks until exams and I need to study for 5 subjects. I feel like I don't have enough time."

### Understanding Complex Topics Examples
- Physics: "Explain electricity using real-world examples for a visual learner preparing for physics exam"
- Chemistry: "Help me understand organic chemistry. I'm burned out from studying and the abstract concepts aren't sticking."
- Math: "I don't understand calculus. I can do the problems but I don't get WHAT I'm actually solving or WHY it works."
- Abstract Concepts: "What is the difference between climate change and weather? I hear these terms but I'm confused about the distinction."

### Study Strategies Examples
- Active Learning: "I've been studying by reading my notes but I feel like I'm not retaining information. What else can I do?"
- Procrastination: "I keep procrastinating on studying. I know I should start but I can't make myself do it."
- Focus Issues: "I get distracted easily when I study. My phone, notifications, everything pulls me away."

## Gap Year Decisions

### Gap Year Examples
- Burnout vs. Momentum: "Should I take a gap year? I'm burned out from Form 6 but worried about losing momentum if I stop studying."
- Productive Gap Year: "I'm considering a gap year but I want it to be productive. Like volunteering or interning instead of just resting."
- Parent Expectations: "What about my parents? They want me to go straight to university. How do I talk to them about a gap year?"
- Planning Gap Year: "If I take a gap year, what should I do? I don't want to waste it just sitting at home."

### Alternative Paths Examples
- Vocational Training: "University isn't the only path. I'm considering vocational training but I'm worried about the stigma."
- Entrepreneurship: "I have a business idea. Should I pursue that instead of going to university?"
- Work First: "I'm thinking about working for a year to save money, then starting university. Is that a good idea?"

## Emotional & Personal Growth

### Anxiety Processing Examples
- KCSE Results: "I'm anxious about my KCSE results. Everyone keeps asking 'what did you get?' and I don't know how to respond."
- Parent Expectations: "I feel pressure to choose a university that will make my parents proud, but I'm not sure what I actually want."
- Comparison: "All my friends seem to have their lives figured out. I'm the only one who doesn't know what to do."
- Future Anxiety: "/frame I'm trying to understand if my anxiety about university is normal or if something is wrong with me."

### Identity & Self-Discovery Examples
- Self-Awareness: "/frame I'm trying to understand my own strengths. I think I'm good at [X] but I'm not sure how that translates to a career."
- Values Discovery: "I don't know what I value in a career. How do I figure out if I care more about money, impact, creativity, or stability?"
- Interests vs. Abilities: "I love art but I'm not very good at it. Should I pursue something I love or something I'm good at?"
- Personality Fit: "I'm shy and prefer working alone. What careers suit that personality type?"

### Confidence & Decision-Making Examples
- Decision Paralysis: "I keep researching options but I can't make a decision. I'm afraid of making the wrong choice."
- Imposter Syndrome: "I got into a competitive program but I feel like I don't deserve it. Everyone else seems smarter than me."
- Fear of Failure: "I want to apply to [competitive program] but I'm afraid of rejection. Should I even try?"
- Trusting Myself: "I second-guess every decision I make. How do I learn to trust my own judgment?"

## Using CIS Agents with Examples

### /frame Examples
- Confusion Clarification: "/frame I'm trying to understand electricity. Here's what I think I know so far: It's about flow of electrons, I've seen Ohm's law. Here's what's confusing me: I don't get the difference between voltage and current, and I can't visualize how circuits actually work."

## Quality Guidelines for Examples

### ‚úÖ Good Examples Are:
- **Specific** - Not "help me study" but "I'm studying for physics exam and confused about voltage vs. current"
- **Relatable** - Reflect real pre-university concerns (anxiety, parents, money, identity)
- **Diverse** - Cover university, career, study, and personal growth domains
- **Emotionally Honest** - Acknowledge anxiety, confusion, pressure, burnout

### ‚ùå Bad Examples Are:
- **Generic** - "Help me with [task]" without context
- **Adult Concerns** - Retirement planning, mortgage decisions, parenting
- **Irrelevant** - Pet poems, random jokes, silly scenarios
- **Inauthentic** - Sound too perfect or polished
