## Session Format Framework

### The 60-Minute Structure

Every Trevor live session follows the same 60-minute format:

| Section | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Opening** | 5 min | Set theme, invite sharing |
| **Student Sharing** | 20 min | Students share experiences |
| **Habit Demonstration** | 15 min | Trevor models habit, students practice |
| **Q&A / Open Conversation** | 15 min | Authentic dialogue, no prepared answers |
| **Closing** | 5 min | Celebrate growth, preview next week |

### Core Principles

1. **"What did YOU notice?" (not "What did you learn?")**
   - Focus on observation, not outcomes
   - Models curiosity over evaluation

2. **Celebrate thinking, not outputs**
   - "That's a great observation about your thinking"
   - Never "That's a good output"

3. **No evaluation, authentic curiosity**
   - No "good" vs "bad" responses
   - Genuine interest in student experience

4. **Model "I don't know" when appropriate**
   - Trevor doesn't have all answers
   - Authentic thinking, not scripting

5. **Volunteers share, no pressure**
   - Students choose to speak, never forced
   - Creates psychological safety

## Trevor Facilitation Guidelines

### Core Philosophy

**"I'm not here to teach you. I'm here to notice with you."**

This philosophy shapes every session:
- Not lectures, but shared discovery
- Not answers, but authentic questions
- Not performance, but genuine thinking
- Not perfection, but real growth

### Session Prep Checklist (30 minutes before each session)

1. **Review the week's theme**
   - What zone shift is happening?
   - Which habit is being reinforced?
   - What's the emotional journey?
   - Which 4 nodes are being filled this week?

2. **Review NotebookLM content for the week**
   - Students have been consuming node podcasts throughout the week
   - Sessions should reference and build on those node concepts
   - Example: "You heard the NotebookLM podcast about 'Context changes everything' - what did YOU notice when you tried it?"
   - **Connection:** Sessions bring node concepts to life through practice and discussion

3. **Prepare your mindset**
   - Set aside any urge to perform or impress
   - Get curious about what students experienced
   - Remember: You're facilitating, not teaching

4. **Gather materials**
   - Any AI demonstrations prepared
   - CIS agent demos ready (check which agents are available this week)
   - Questions you genuinely have

5. **Check logistics**
   - Time zone confirmation
   - Cluster size (usually 12-15 students)
   - Technical setup (Discord voice, screen sharing if needed)

### During Session: Key Practices

**1. Ask "What did YOU notice?" (not "What did you learn?")**

Why: Notice is about observation, learn is about outcomes. We care about their thinking process, not results.

**2. Celebrate thinking, never outputs**

Good: "That's a great observation about how context changed the response"
Bad: "That's a great prompt you wrote"

**3. No evaluation of "good" vs "bad" responses**

Every response is valid data about their experience. Celebrate the thinking, don't grade the answer.

**4. Model not-knowing**

It's powerful when Trevor says:
- "I don't know - that's a great question"
- "I'm figuring this out too"
- "Let's explore that together"

**5. Volunteers share, never forced**

If silence happens:
- "Anyone want to share? No pressure."
- "If you'd rather think silently, that's okay too."
- Never call on specific students

**6. Authentic curiosity over prepared content**

Students can tell when you're genuinely interested vs. when you're following a script. Real questions create real engagement.

### Common Situations & How to Handle

**Situation 1: Long silence after asking for sharing**

Trevor: "I know sharing can feel vulnerable. If you'd rather just think silently for a moment, that's okay. I'll wait."

[Wait 10 seconds of silence]

Trevor: "If no one wants to share right now, that's totally fine. These reflections are personal. You can always share in Discord in writing if that feels easier."

**Situation 2: Student gives a "wrong" or confused answer**

Trevor: "That's a really interesting observation. Tell me more about what you noticed."

[Student explains]

Trevor: "I hear what you're saying. That confusion is actually normal - this stuff takes time. You're on track."

**Situation 3: Student says "I didn't do the practice this week"**

Trevor: "Thanks for being honest. That happens. Life gets busy. What would help you engage next week?"

[Student responds]

Trevor: "That's a great idea. Let's make that happen. No judgment - we're all learning."

**Situation 4: Student dominates conversation, others aren't speaking**

Trevor: "These are great insights. Let's hear from someone who hasn't shared yet - who else noticed something different this week?"

**Situation 5: Technical difficulty with AI demo**

Trevor: "Well, that didn't work as planned. This is actually perfect - it shows that AI isn't magic. Sometimes it doesn't do what we expect. That's why we iterate. Let me try a different approach..."

[Model iteration live]

**Situation 6: Emotional moment - student gets teary or vulnerable**

Trevor: [Acknowledges gently] "That's powerful. Thank you for trusting us with that."

[Then move forward gently, don't dwell]
"Let's take a breath. You're not alone in feeling that way. These transformations are real, and they're emotional. That's okay."

### After Session: 10-Minute Reflection

After each session, Trevor should reflect:

1. **What surprised me?**
   - Any unexpected student insights?
   - What themes emerged?

2. **What felt authentic?**
   - When did I feel most connected?
   - What created real engagement?

3. **What felt forced?**
   - Any moments that didn't land?
   - What should I adjust next time?

4. **Students to follow up with**
   - Anyone seem stuck or confused?
   - Anyone need outreach?
